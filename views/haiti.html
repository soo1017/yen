HAITI

Officially the Republic of Haiti and formerly called Hayti is a sovereign state located on the island of Hispaniola in the Greater Antilles archipelago of the Caribbean Sea.

Haiti is 27,750 square kilometres (10,714 sq mi) in size and has an estimated 10.8 million people, making it the most populous country in the Caribbean Community and the second-most populous country in the Caribbean as a whole.

The poorest country in the Americas and the 3rd poorest country in the world, Majority of Haiti’s 10 million people live on less than $3 a day.

Roughly three-quarters of Haitians are either unemployed or trying to make ends meet in the informal economy and 75% of Haiti’s people live in abject poverty.

HAITI CHILDREN

There are very few public schools in Haiti. Most schools are private and parents must pay for them. The schools may not have running water or toilets. They may not have enough books or even qualified teachers. Many children never learn to read

Haiti has the highest infant mortality rate in the Americas: Preventable illnesses, such as cholera, tuberculosis and pneumonia are the main causes of deaths in children. Around 100,000 Haitian children aged between 0-17 have lost parental care because of AIDS.

Only one in five children starts secondary school. Many drop out of school in order to work. According to the United Nations, roughly 300,000 children in Haiti work in conditions similar to slavery.

After Hurricane Matthew in 2016, an additional 130,000 children were out of school and 10,000 children were in need of protection from exploitation and abuse.
